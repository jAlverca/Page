Research Article Summaries: 5G/6G Networks, Security, and Slicing

1. Sistemas de Infoentretenimiento: Situación actual y perspectivas de investigación futuras respecto a las tecnologías 5G / Infotainment systems: Current status and future research perspectives toward 5G technologies

Authors: Nadia Karina Gamboa Rosales, José María Celaya-Padilla, Carlos E. Galván-Tejada, Jorge I. Galván-Tejada, Huitzilopoztli Luna-García, Hamurabi Gamboa-Rosales, José Ricardo López Robles.

Publication: Iberoamerican Journal of Science Measurement and Communication; Vol. 2, No. 1, 1-16. DOI: 10.47909/ijsmc.147 (2022)

Summary:

Objective: (1) To identify and visualize research on infotainment systems and 5G technologies. (2) To describe their current condition based on scientific literature through bibliometric indicators (publication output, citations, authorship, geographic distribution).

Design/Methodology/Approach: Data extracted from Scopus (temporal coverage 2016-2022). Methodological procedures in two phases:

  1. Evaluation of productivity and impact through bibliometric indicators.
  2. Examination of relevant topics using bibliometric mapping with SciMAT software.

Results/Discussion: 128 research topics were identified. Prominent topics include: Signal-to-Interference-Ratio (SIR), Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V), Mobile-Security, Energy-Utilization, Mobility-Management, Resource-Allocation-Techniques, Roads-and-Streets, Vehicular-Applications, Internet-of-Things (IoT), Intelligent-Connected-and-Autonomous-Vehicles, and Software-Defined-Networking (SDN). A strong link was evidenced between telecommunications, mobility, and electronics sectors, with the vehicle considered a deployment element.

Conclusions: Infotainment systems and 5G technologies are evolving. This evolution is driven individually by each technology and by their synergy and application in vehicles. Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) communication is a key catalyst in the evolution of mobility.

Keywords (Palabras clave): Inteligencia Artificial; Infoentretenimiento; Vehículo conectado; Telecomunicaciones; Comunicaciones móviles; Tecnologías 5G. (Artificial Intelligence; Infotainment; Connected Vehicle; Telecommunications; Mobile communications; 5G technologies).

2. Security Requirements and Challenges of 6G Technologies and Applications

Authors: Shimaa A. Abdel Hakeem, Hanan H. Hussein, HyungWon Kim.

Publication: Sensors 2022, 22, 1969. DOI: 10.3390/s22051969

Summary:

Context: 6G technology is expected around 2030, offering hyper-connectivity and extending mobile communication possibilities. It will incorporate technologies like post-quantum cryptography (PQC), AI/ML, enhanced edge computing, molecular communication, THz, VLC, and DLT (e.g., blockchain).

Problem: These 6G developments necessitate a re-evaluation of traditional security methods. New approaches for authentication, encryption, access control, communication, and malicious activity detection are needed to satisfy higher security requirements and ensure trustworthiness and privacy.

Paper's Contribution:

  • Provides insights into critical problems related to security, privacy, and trust issues of 6G networks.
  • Clarifies standard technologies and security challenges for each technology.
  • Introduces the 6G security architecture and improvements over 5G.
  • Discusses security issues of the 6G physical layer, AI/ML layers, and proposed solutions.
  • Summarizes security evolution in legacy mobile networks and essential 6G application services with their security requirements.
  • Provides a discussion of 6G networks’ trustworthiness and solutions.

Keywords: 6G security; privacy; new challenges; security architecture; security threats; physical layer security; AI/ML security.

3. ML-Based 5G Network Slicing Security: A Comprehensive Survey

Authors: Ramraj Dangi, Akshay Jadhav, Gaurav Choudhary, Nicola Dragoni, Manas Kumar Mishra, Praveen Lalwani.

Publication: Future Internet 2022, 14, 116. DOI: 10.3390/fi14040116

Summary:

Context: 5G networks support diverse services like eMBB, eMTC, and URLLC. Network slicing partitions the physical network into multiple virtual networks ("slices") to customize services and meet user needs.

Problem: Network slicing introduces critical security concerns as adversaries evolve and employ new attack strategies.

Paper's Focus:

  • Security issues arising during the network slice lifecycle.
  • Application of Machine Learning (ML) and Deep Learning (DL) algorithms in various phases of network slicing: planning and design, construction and deployment, monitoring, fault detection, and security.
  • Outlines the 5G network slicing concept, its layers, architectural framework, and prevention of attacks, threats, and issues.
  • Provides a comparison of existing surveys and maps out taxonomies for ML solutions for different application parameters and network functions.

Keywords: 5G network; network slicing; security; threats; machine learning.

4. Enhancing Network Slicing Security: Machine Learning, Software-Defined Networking, and Network Functions Virtualization-Driven Strategies

Authors: José Cunha, Pedro Ferreira, Eva M. Castro, Paula Cristina Oliveira, Maria João Nicolau, Iván Núñez, Xosé Ramon Sousa, Carlos Serôdio.

Publication: Future Internet 2024, 16, 226. DOI: 10.3390/fi16070226

Summary:

Context: Rapid development of 5G and anticipation of 6G technologies facilitate highly customizable network environments through network slicing.

Problem: Network slicing, despite its benefits, introduces significant security vulnerabilities against sophisticated cyber threats.

Paper's Focus:

  • Explores the application of cutting-edge technologies: Artificial Intelligence (AI), specifically Machine Learning (ML), Software-Defined Networking (SDN), and Network Functions Virtualization (NFV) in crafting advanced security solutions for network slicing.
  • Analyzes AI’s predictive threat detection and automated response capabilities for service integrity and resilience.
  • Scrutinizes SDN and NFV for their ability to enforce flexible security policies and manage network functionalities dynamically.
  • Identifies critical research gaps and proposes innovative solutions, advocating for a holistic security strategy integrating ML, SDN, and NFV.
  • Concludes with future research directions for robust, scalable, and efficient security frameworks.

Keywords: network security; SDN; NFV; ML; network slicing.

5. Detection and Prediction of Inter-Slice Handover DDoS Attacks in 5G and Beyond Networks Using P4 and Deep Learning

Authors: R. Mohammadifar, R. Javidan, R. Akbari.

Publication: International Journal of Engineering, Transactions B: Applications Vol. 38 No. 08, (August 2025) 1984-2003. DOI: 10.5829/ije.2025.38.08b.21

Summary:

Problem: Securing 5G and beyond (6G) networks against Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks during inter-slice handovers.

Proposed Solution: A hybrid model based on P4 programmable switches and the Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) algorithm to detect and predict such attacks with high accuracy and low latency. P4 enables real-time extraction of key Quality of Service (QoS) parameters (packet loss rate, latency, priority) for traffic analysis and attack detection.

Results:

  • DDoS detection accuracy: 98.63%
  • Sensitivity: 98.53%
  • F1 score: 98.58%
  • Legitimate slice prediction accuracy: 98.7%
  • False Positive Rate (FPR): < 2.1%
  • Total system delay for detection and decision-making: < 350 milliseconds (suitable for latency-sensitive applications like URLLC).
  • Scalability: Maintains >90% detection accuracy and <500ms delay with up to 15 switches and 4 slices under high traffic.

Conclusion: The research highlights the effectiveness of combining deep learning with P4 for enhancing security and scalability in advanced networks, providing a robust framework for next-generation network security.

Keywords: Software-Defined Networks; Distributed Denial of Service Attack; Inter-Slice Handover; Deep Learning; Fifth Generation; Sixth Generation.
